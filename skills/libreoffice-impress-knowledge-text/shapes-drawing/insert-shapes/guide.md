# Insert and Format Shapes

1. Open LibreOffice Impress and navigate to the slide where you want to add shapes.
2. Select **View > Toolbars > Drawing** to ensure the Drawing toolbar is visible.
3. Click the rectangle, ellipse, or arrow tool from the Drawing toolbar.
4. Click and drag on the slide to draw the shape.
5. To draw a perfect circle or square, hold **Shift** while dragging.
6. Right-click the shape and select **Area** to open the Area dialog.
7. Under the **Area** tab, choose a fill type: **None**, **Color**, **Gradient**, **Hatching**, or **Bitmap**.
8. Select a color or style from the list and click **OK**.
9. To fill a shape with an image, click the **Bitmap** tab in the Area dialog.
10. Click **Add/Import** to browse your file system and select an image file.
11. Select the imported image from the bitmap list and adjust tiling/size settings as needed.
12. Click **OK** to apply the image fill.
13. To change the border, right-click the shape and select **Line**.
14. In the Line dialog, set the **Style**, **Color**, and **Width**, then click **OK**.
15. To resize the shape, click to select it and drag the corner handles.
16. To move the shape, click and drag it to the desired position on the slide.
