# Slide Dimensions & Page Setup (LibreOffice Impress 7.3.7)

Open the dialog via **Slide > Slide Properties...** (or **Slide > Slide Size...** depending on your menu set), then click the **Slide** tab. This is your one-stop shop for dimensions, orientation, and margins.

Read the screenshot `fig01.png` in this directory — you will see the Slide Properties dialog with the Slide tab selected, showing Paper Format, Width, Height, Orientation, Margins, and Layout Settings fields.

The **Paper format** dropdown gives you common presets — Letter, A4, A3, and so on. For a standard widescreen presentation, pick a width of 33.867 cm by 19.05 cm (roughly 16:9). The classic 4:3 ratio uses 25.4 cm × 19.05 cm. You can also type any custom dimensions directly into the **Width** and **Height** fields for things like large-format posters or signage.

**Orientation** has two groups: one for the slides themselves and one for notes and handouts. Flip slides to **Portrait** when you're designing a vertical sign or flyer, and keep **Landscape** for normal presentations. Notes and handouts can be set independently, which is handy if you want portrait handouts from a landscape deck.

The **Margins** fields (left, right, top, bottom) control the non-printable border area. For on-screen presentations these rarely matter, but tighten or loosen them if you're printing or exporting to PDF for physical media.

Under **Page numbering format**, you can switch between Arabic numerals, Roman numerals, or letters — useful when your slide numbers need to match a specific document convention.

When you change the slide size on a deck that already has content, Impress will ask whether to **scale** existing objects to fit the new dimensions or keep them at their current size. Scaling is usually the safer bet unless you have precisely positioned elements you don't want distorted.

Hit **OK** and the change applies to *all* slides in the presentation — there's no per-slide sizing in Impress.
