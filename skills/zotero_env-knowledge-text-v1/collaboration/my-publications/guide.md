# Publishing with My Publications (Zotero 7)

My Publications lets you build a public bibliography on your zotero.org profile page, showcasing your own research with optional file sharing. Think of it as your personal academic showcase, right inside Zotero.

To get started, find the **My Publications** collection in the left panel under **My Library** — it sits alongside Duplicate Items, Unfiled Items, and Trash. Drag any item you want to share into that collection.

Once you drop an item in, a helper window pops up. Here you can toggle **Include files** and **Include notes** to control what gets published alongside the bibliographic record. Links to external copies (your website, a repository, or the publisher) are always shared. If there's no attachment or link, Zotero falls back to the item's DOI or URL field. Check the box confirming **I created this work and have the rights to distribute included files**, then click **Next: Sharing**.

If you're sharing files, you'll pick a distribution model: **No, only publish my work on zotero.org** keeps all rights reserved, **Yes, under a Creative Commons license** lets others redistribute with attribution, or **Yes, and place my work in the public domain** waives all rights via CC0 (and that's irreversible, so read the fine print).

Choosing Creative Commons takes you to one more screen where you decide whether to allow adaptations (no, yes with share-alike, or yes) and whether to permit commercial use. Zotero assembles the matching CC license automatically and displays it with your shared files. Hit **Add to My Publications** and you're done.

Only share files you actually have distribution rights for — check your publisher agreements or look them up on SHERPA/RoMEO. Pre-prints and author manuscripts are often fair game even when the final version isn't. You can adjust what's visible anytime by revisiting the My Publications collection.
