# Adding Notes (Zotero 7)

Zotero lets you attach rich-text notes to items in your library. Notes sync with your item metadata and don't count against your file storage quota — so use them freely.

**Child notes** are attached to a specific item. Select an item in the center pane, then click the **New Note** button at the top of the center pane and choose **Add Child Note**. Alternatively, open the **Notes** tab in the right-hand pane and click **Add**, or just right-click the item and select **Add Note**. The note editor opens in the right-hand pane, and your text saves automatically as you type. If you need more room, click **Edit in a separate window** at the bottom of the editor to pop it out.

**Standalone notes** aren't tied to any particular item — they live alongside your other library entries. To create one, click the **New Note** button and choose **New Standalone Note**.

The note editor includes a formatting toolbar with bold, italic, underline, strikethrough, sub/superscript, text color, block quotes, lists, and links. A **Paragraph** dropdown lets you set heading levels. You can embed images by dragging them from your filesystem or pasting them in; embedded images sync across devices but do count against your storage quota.

At the bottom of every note editor you'll find **Tags** and **Related** fields — notes are first-class items, so you can tag them and link them to other items just like anything else in your library.

To search within a note, use **Cmd/Ctrl+F** or the **Find and Replace** button in the editor. Notes also appear in Zotero's general search results.
