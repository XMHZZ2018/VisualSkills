# Attaching Files and Web Pages (Zotero 7)

Files in Zotero live as either *child attachments* under a regular item or as *standalone items*. Always prefer child attachments — standalone files can't be cited, searched properly, or used with My Publications. If you drop a bare PDF into your library, Zotero will try to retrieve its metadata and create a parent item automatically. When that fails, right-click the PDF, choose **Create Parent Item**, and enter a DOI or ISBN (or click **Manual Entry** as a last resort).

Zotero stores files in two ways. **Stored files** (the default) are copied into your Zotero data directory, synced across devices, and deleted when you remove the attachment — this is the recommended approach. **Linked files** are just pointers to files elsewhere on disk; they don't sync, can't be used in group libraries or mobile apps, and require more manual management.

To attach a file to an existing item, select the item and click the **Add Attachment** (paperclip) button at the top of the center column, then pick **Attach Stored Copy of File…** or **Attach Link to File…**. You can also right-click the item and choose **Add Attachment** for the same options. To link to a web page or app URL, choose **Attach Link to URI…** and enter an `http://`, `onenote://`, or similar address.

For standalone files, click the **New Item** (green plus) button and select **Store Copy of File…** or **Link to File…**.

Drag-and-drop works too: drag a file from Finder or Explorer onto an item in the center pane to add it as a child attachment, or drop it into empty space to create a standalone item. To create a *linked* attachment instead of a copy, hold **Cmd+Option** (Mac) or **Ctrl+Shift** (Windows/Linux) while dropping.

The Zotero Connector in your browser automatically saves PDFs and snapshots when you click the save button on a supported page. To manually snapshot any page, right-click the Zotero save button in the browser toolbar and select **Save to Zotero (Web Page with Snapshot)**. Snapshot saving can be toggled in **Edit > Settings > General**.

To open an attached file, double-click it in the center pane (or right-click and choose **View PDF** / **View File**). To reveal the actual file on disk, right-click and select **Show File**.
