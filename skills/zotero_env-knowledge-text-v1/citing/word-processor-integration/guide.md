# Word Processor Integration (Zotero 7)

Zotero's word processor plugins for Microsoft Word, LibreOffice, and Google Docs let you insert live citations and auto-generated bibliographies right inside your document. The plugins ship with Zotero and install automatically the first time you launch it. If they go missing, reinstall from **Zotero > Settings > Cite > Word Processor Plugins**.

Once installed, you'll find a **Zotero** tab in Word's ribbon. The key buttons there are **Add/Edit Citation**, **Add/Edit Bibliography**, **Document Preferences**, **Refresh**, and **Unlink Citations**.

To cite something, click **Add/Edit Citation**. A search bar appears — start typing an author name, title, or year, and matching items from your library pop up instantly. Click one (or press Enter) to select it; it becomes a shaded bubble in the bar. Press Enter again to drop the citation into your document. To build a multi-source citation like "(Smith 1776, Marx 1867)", just keep searching and selecting additional items before pressing Enter.

Need a page number? After selecting an item, type `p.45` or just `45` right in the search bar — Zotero adds it as a locator. For a prefix like "cf." or a suffix like "see also", click the citation bubble to open cite options, where you'll find **Prefix**, **Suffix**, and **Suppress Author** fields. Checking **Suppress Author** gives you a narrative style like "According to Smith (1776)".

For the bibliography, place your cursor where you want it and click **Add/Edit Bibliography**. Zotero builds it from every citation in the document and keeps it updated automatically. To add uncited items or remove specific entries, click **Add/Edit Bibliography** again to open the editor and use the arrows to adjust.

Open **Document Preferences** to change the citation style, switch between footnotes and endnotes, choose the citation language, or toggle between **Fields** and **Bookmarks** storage (stick with Fields unless you're collaborating across Word and LibreOffice).

When you've finished your manuscript, click **Refresh** to pull in any metadata changes from your library. Only use **Unlink Citations** on a final copy — it strips all Zotero field codes and is irreversible, but it lets you make last-mile formatting tweaks to the bibliography as plain text.
