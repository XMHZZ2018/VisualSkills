# Using Synonyms and the Thesaurus (LibreOffice Writer 7.3.7)

Need a better word? Writer has you covered with two quick ways to find alternatives: the Synonyms submenu for a fast swap, and the full Thesaurus for deeper exploration.

**Quick synonyms via right-click.** Select or place your cursor on a word, then right-click and point to **Synonyms** on the context menu. A submenu pops up with a short list of alternative words and phrases. Just click one and it instantly replaces the highlighted word in your document — no dialog boxes, no fuss.

See `fig01.png`.

**Opening the full Thesaurus.** When the short list isn't enough, go deeper. Right-click the word, point to **Synonyms**, and click **Thesaurus…** at the bottom of the submenu. You can also get there from the menu bar via **Tools > Thesaurus**, or just press **Ctrl+F7**.

In the Thesaurus dialog, you'll see a more extensive list of alternative words and phrases organized by meaning. Browse through the list, select the word or phrase you like, and click **Replace** to make the substitution right in your document.

**A few tips.** The synonyms and thesaurus features depend on the language dictionaries you have installed, so if you're not seeing results, check that the appropriate language pack is set up under **Tools > Options > Language Settings**. Also, the Thesaurus works best with common base forms of words — if you're not getting good results, try looking up the root word instead of a conjugated or plural form.
