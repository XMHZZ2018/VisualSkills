# Using Grids and Guides (GIMP 2.10)

Every image in GIMP has a grid — it's just hidden by default. Toggle it on with **View > Show Grid**, and you'll see crosshair markers at every intersection, spaced 10 pixels apart. To customize the look for the current image, head to **Image > Configure Grid…** where you can pick from five line styles (dots, crosshairs, dashed, double-dashed, or solid), change colors, and adjust spacing and offset.

Once the grid is visible, turn on **View > Snap to Grid** and your pointer will magnetically lock to the nearest grid line within 8 pixels — handy for precise alignment. Snapping works even with the grid hidden, though that's rarely useful.

Guides offer more flexibility. Click on a ruler (top or side of the canvas) and drag inward to pull out a blue dashed line you can position anywhere. For exact placement, use **Image > Guides > New Guide…** and type in a pixel position and direction. You can create as many as you need.

To reposition a guide, grab the **Move** tool (or press **M**) and drag it. Drag a guide back onto the ruler to delete it. To clear everything at once, use **Image > Guides > Remove all Guides**. Hold **Shift** while moving to move your content without disturbing the guides.

Enable **View > Snap to Guides** and layers or selections will stick to nearby guides like magnets — perfect for lining up elements. If guides get visually distracting, temporarily hide them with **View > Show Guides**.

If you need a grid baked into the actual image pixels (not just an overlay), use the **Filters > Light and Shadow > Grid** plugin, which renders real grid lines onto your canvas with even more style options than the display grid.
