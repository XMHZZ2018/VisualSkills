# Improving Photo Composition (GIMP 2.10)

When your photo is slightly tilted, grab the **Rotate** tool from the Toolbox (or press **Shift+R**). Make sure **Transform Layer** is selected in the Tool Options. Click and drag inside the image to rotate a preview grid until things look level, then press **Enter**. For easier alignment, switch Transform Direction to **Backward (Corrective)** in the Tool Options — this lets you align the grid *to* the tilt rather than compensating against it.

Avoid rotating twice to fine-tune; each rotation slightly blurs the image. Instead, undo with **Ctrl+Z** and redo the rotation with a better angle in one pass. Enable the preview option in Tool Options to nail it on the first attempt.

After rotating, you'll have empty triangular corners. Crop them away using the **Crop** tool (**Shift+C**). Click and drag to define the area you want to keep, then press **Enter** to apply. If **Delete cropped pixels** is unchecked in Tool Options, the hidden pixels remain — only the canvas boundary changes.

Even without rotation issues, cropping can dramatically improve a photo. Try the "rule of thirds": position your subject roughly one-third from an edge, both horizontally and vertically, for a more dynamic composition. Shoot a little wide when you can — it's easier to crop in post than to wish you'd captured more.
