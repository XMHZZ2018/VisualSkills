# Using Collections and Tags (Zotero 7)

Zotero gives you two complementary ways to organize your library: **collections** (hierarchical folders) and **tags** (flat labels you attach to items). Think of collections like playlists — the same item can live in several collections without being duplicated. Tags, on the other hand, let you slice across collections with fine-grained labels like "to-read" or "methods." Both are worth using; experiment to see what fits your workflow.

## Collections

To create a collection, click the **New Collection…** button at the top of the left pane, or right-click **My Library** and choose **New Collection…**. You can nest collections by right-clicking an existing one and picking **New Subcollection…**, or just drag one collection onto another.

See `fig01.png`.

Adding items to a collection is as simple as dragging them from the center pane onto the collection in the left pane. This *copies* the item into the collection — it stays wherever it was before, too. To *move* an item instead (removing it from the original collection), hold **Cmd** (Mac) or **Shift** (Windows/Linux) while dragging. Items saved from the browser automatically go into whichever collection is selected at the time.

To remove items from a collection without trashing them, select the items and press **Delete** (or right-click and choose **Remove Item(s) from Collection…**). The items remain in **My Library** and any other collections. Deleting a collection itself — via right-click > **Delete Collection…** — likewise keeps its items in the library. If you want to nuke both the collection and its contents, choose **Delete Collection and Items…** instead.

A few special collections are worth knowing about: **Unfiled Items** surfaces anything not in any collection, **Duplicate Items** flags potential duplicates for merging, and **My Publications** lets you share your own work publicly. You can also toggle **View > Show Items from Subcollections** to roll child-collection items up into the parent view.

## Tags

The **tag selector** lives at the bottom of the left pane and shows every tag on the currently visible items. Click any tag to filter the center pane down to items carrying that tag — click again to deselect. The search box at the bottom of the selector lets you quickly find a specific tag by name.

See `fig02.png`.

To tag an item, select it, open the **Tags** tab in the right pane, and click **Add**. Start typing and Zotero will autocomplete from existing tags — press **Tab** or **Enter** to accept a suggestion. You can also drag items onto a tag in the tag selector to bulk-apply it.

Right-clicking a tag in the selector gives you **Rename Tag…** and **Delete Tag…**, both of which apply globally across your library. Renaming a tag to match an existing one merges them — handy for cleaning up duplicates.

For tags you use constantly, right-click a tag and choose **Assign Color…** to give it a color. Colored tags show as small squares next to item titles in the center pane and pin themselves to the top of the tag selector. Each colored tag also gets a number key (1–9) so you can toggle it on selected items with a single keystroke. Emoji tags work too — use an emoji as the tag name and it'll display inline instead of a colored square.

See `fig03.png` for the colored tag squares next to item titles.

When you save items from the web, Zotero may pull in **automatic tags** from the source (like subject headings from library catalogs). These appear with a red icon in the Tags tab. If they clutter your selector, click the multi-color button in the tag selector's corner and uncheck **Show Automatic**, or disable them entirely in **Edit > Settings > General** by unchecking **Automatically tag items with keywords and subject headings**.
