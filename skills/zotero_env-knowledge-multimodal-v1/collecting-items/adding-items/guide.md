# Adding Items to Your Library (Zotero 7)

The fastest way to get items into Zotero is through the **Zotero Connector**, a browser extension available for Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari. Install it alongside the desktop app — without it, you're missing Zotero's best trick.

## Saving from the browser

As you browse, the Connector's save button in your browser toolbar automatically detects what you're looking at. Land on a journal article page and the icon becomes a little article; visit a library catalog entry for a book and it switches to a book icon. Just click it, and Zotero pulls in the full bibliographic record — and often the PDF too, if one is accessible.

See `fig01.png`.

When you're on a page with multiple results — say, a Google Scholar search — the save button turns into a folder icon. Click it and you'll get a checkbox dialog where you can cherry-pick exactly which items to import.

See `fig02.png`.

After saving, a popup lets you redirect the item to a specific collection or library and add tags on the fly.

## Adding by identifier

Already have a DOI, ISBN, PubMed ID, or arXiv ID? Click the **Add Item by Identifier** button (the magic-wand icon in the toolbar), paste in the identifier, and hit **Enter**. Zotero looks it up against CrossRef, Library of Congress, PubMed, and other registries and creates a fully populated item. You can even paste multiple identifiers at once, separated by spaces or commas.

See `fig03.png`.

## Handling PDFs and files

Whenever possible, save from the article's abstract page rather than the PDF itself — you'll get better metadata. But if all you have is a PDF (received via email, for instance), just drag it into Zotero's center pane. Zotero will try to retrieve metadata automatically and create a proper parent item. If that fails, right-click the standalone attachment, choose **Create Parent Item**, and enter a DOI or ISBN. As a last resort, pick **Manual Entry** from that same dialog.

## Manual entry

Zotero tries hard to spare you from typing metadata by hand, but sometimes a source simply isn't online. In that case, click the green **New Item** button at the top of the center pane, pick the item type from the dropdown (the full list is under **More**), and fill in the fields in the right-hand pane. Changes save automatically.

## Always verify your records

No matter how you add items, give the metadata a quick once-over. Imported records can have missing authors, wrong journal names, or title-cased titles that should be sentence case. Fix it once in Zotero and every future citation will be correct, in any style.
