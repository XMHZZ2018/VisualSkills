# Word Processor Integration (Zotero 7)

Zotero's word processor plugins let you insert live citations and bibliographies directly into Microsoft Word, LibreOffice, or Google Docs. The plugins ship with Zotero and install automatically the first time you launch it. If they go missing, reinstall them from **Zotero > Settings > Cite > Word Processor Plugins**.

Once installed, you'll find a **Zotero** tab in Word's ribbon. It has five buttons: **Add/Edit Citation**, **Add/Edit Bibliography**, **Document Preferences**, **Refresh**, and **Unlink Citations**. That toolbar is your whole workflow.

To cite something, click **Add/Edit Citation**. A search bar drops down — start typing an author name, title keyword, or year, and matching items from your library appear instantly. Click a result (or arrow-key to it and press Enter) to add it as a bubble in the bar. Press Enter again to drop the citation into your document.

See `fig01.png`.

Need to cite a specific page? Before you press Enter to insert, just type `p.34` or `:34` right in the search bar — Zotero picks it up. For more control, click the citation bubble to open the cite options popover, where you can set a locator type (page, chapter, volume), add a prefix like "cf." or a suffix like "see also," and check **Suppress Author** for narrative citations like "According to Smith (1776)."

See `fig02.png`.

To bundle multiple sources into one citation — say "(Smith 1776; Marx 1867)" — just keep searching and selecting items in the same dialog before pressing Enter. Zotero sorts them automatically per the style's rules; drag the bubbles to override the order if needed.

Click **Add/Edit Bibliography** to place a bibliography at your cursor. From that point on it's fully automatic: every citation you add or remove updates the bibliography in place. If you need to include uncited sources or exclude certain items (like personal communications), click the same button again to open the **Edit Bibliography** window and use the arrows to add or remove entries.

Before you start writing, hit **Document Preferences** to pick your citation style (APA, Chicago, etc.), set the language, choose between footnotes and endnotes for note-based styles, and decide whether to store citations as **Fields** or **Bookmarks** (stick with Fields unless you're collaborating across Word and LibreOffice).

See `fig03.png`.

If you update item metadata in your Zotero library — fixing a title typo, for example — click **Refresh** in the toolbar and every citation and bibliography entry in the document updates to match. When your manuscript is truly final and you want plain text, click **Unlink Citations** to strip all field codes. This is irreversible, so do it on a copy.
