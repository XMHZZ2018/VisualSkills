# Working with Groups (Zotero 7)

Zotero groups let you share libraries with collaborators — whether that's a seminar class, a lab team, or a loose network of researchers tracking a shared topic. Group libraries live separately from your personal **My Library**, which means they also double as a handy way to maintain distinct collections within a single Zotero profile. There's no cap on group membership, and your full storage subscription covers both personal and group libraries.

To create a group, go to **File → New Library → New Group…** in the Zotero desktop client. Alternatively, head to the [groups page](https://www.zotero.org/groups) on zotero.org and hit **Create a New Group**. You'll choose from three types: **Private** (completely hidden, no public footprint), **Public, Closed Membership** (visible page but invite-only), or **Public, Open Membership** (anyone can join instantly). Note that open public groups don't support file sharing — if your team needs to share PDFs, go with private or closed.

Once the group exists, manage it from its settings pages on zotero.org. The **Members Settings** page is where you invite people (look for **Send More Invitations**), assign roles, and promote members to admin. There are three roles: regular member, administrator, and owner. A practical tip: if this is a lab or organization group, create a dedicated Zotero account to act as the owner so you're never locked out if someone leaves.

Under **Library Settings**, you control who can read, edit, and upload files. Reading can be open to "anyone on the internet" or restricted to members. Editing and file access can be limited to admins only — useful for curated bibliographies where you don't want everyone rearranging things.

With syncing enabled, your group libraries appear in Zotero's left pane right below **My Library**. You can drag items from your personal library into a group, create collections by right-clicking the group name and choosing **New Collection…**, and browse everything your collaborators have added. Just remember: items in a group library are independent copies — edits there won't update your personal copy, and vice versa.

Every group also gets a web page on zotero.org. For public groups this is a portal showcasing the library to the world; for private groups it gives members browser-based access to the shared collection from anywhere.
