# Formatting slides (LibreOffice Impress 7.3)

Use master slides to create a consistent look across slides instead of formatting each slide manually.

## Slide Setup

**Via dialog:**
1. Select **Normal** view in the Workspace.
2. Open the Slide Properties dialog: **Slide > Slide Properties** or right-click empty area on slide > **Slide Properties**.
3. Configure options in the **Slide**, **Background**, and **Transparency** pages.
4. Click **OK**.

**Via Sidebar:**
1. Select **Normal** view.
2. Click **Properties** in the Sidebar > click **Slide** to open the Slide panel.
3. Set **Format** (size), **Orientation**, **Background**, and **Master Slide** from the drop-down lists.
4. Optionally enable/disable **Master Background** or **Master Objects**.
5. Click **Master View** to preview the master slide.

## Selecting Master Slides

1. Select **Normal** view.
2. In the Sidebar, click **Master Slides** to open the Master Slides deck.
3. Choose a master slide from **Used in This Presentation**, **Recently Used**, or **Available for Use**.
4. Right-click the master slide and select:
   - **Apply to All Slides** — applies to entire presentation.
   - **Apply to Selected Slides** — applies to selected slides only.

## Changing Master Slides

1. Select **Normal** view and select the target slide in the Slides pane.
2. Right-click blank area on the slide > **Change Slide Master**.
3. Select a master slide from the **Select a Slide Design** preview box.
   - If not listed, click **Load**, choose a **Category** and **Template**, then click **OK**.
4. Click **OK** to apply.

## Changing Slide Background

1. Select **Normal** view and select a slide.
2. Open **Slide Properties**: **Slide > Slide Properties** or right-click empty area > **Slide Properties**.
3. Click **Background**, select background type and options.
4. Click **OK**.

> Note: Only one slide's background can be changed at a time. Use master slides to change multiple slides simultaneously.

## Choosing Slide Layout

After creating a new slide, select a predefined layout to match the slide contents. If no layout fits, layout elements can be rearranged, duplicated, resized, or deleted as needed.
