# Flowcharts (LibreOffice Impress 7.3.7)

A flowchart in Impress is really just a combination of two things: shapes from the Flowchart category and connectors that link them together. Once you know where to find both, the whole process clicks into place.

Start by grabbing your shapes. On the Drawing toolbar, click the triangle ▼ next to the shape icon and pick **Flowchart** from the list — you'll see the usual suspects like Process, Decision, Terminator, and more. Click and drag on the slide to place each shape, then double-click it to type a label directly inside.

To wire shapes together, click the triangle ▼ next to **Connectors** on the Drawing toolbar to open the Connectors sub-toolbar. Pick a connector type — **Standard** connectors use right-angle bends, **Line** connectors create angled segments, **Straight** draws a direct line, and **Curved** uses smooth Bézier curves. Hover over a shape and you'll see small crosses at the edges — those are glue points. Click one, hold, and drag to a glue point on another shape to snap the connector in place.

Visual reference: two shapes connected by multiple connector types — standard right-angle, straight, and curved — with glue points visible on both objects.

The beauty of connectors is that they stay attached. Move or resize a shape and the connector follows automatically, which saves a ton of cleanup when you're rearranging your flow.

If the default glue points aren't where you need them, open the Glue Points toolbar via **View > Toolbars > Glue Points**. Click **Insert Glue Point**, then click on a shape to place a custom attachment point. You can set its exit direction (left, right, top, bottom) and control whether it maintains its relative position when the shape is resized using **Glue Point Relative**.

To change a connector's routing or style after it's drawn, right-click it and select **Connector** to open the Connector dialog. From there you can switch the connector type, adjust line skew, and set spacing values for each end.

Visual reference: the Connector dialog with Type dropdown, Line Skew fields, and Line Spacing settings, alongside a live preview of the connector between two shapes.

For quick alignment, hold *Shift* while drawing shapes to constrain them to squares or circles, and hold *Ctrl* (⌘ on macOS) to snap to the grid. Keep your flow running top-to-bottom or left-to-right, and your diagram will practically read itself.
