# Creating Presentations (LibreOffice Impress 7.3.7)

By default, Impress opens with a Select a Template dialog. If you want a blank slate instead, click **Cancel** to skip it and land on an empty slide. To stop that dialog from appearing altogether, go to **Tools > Options > LibreOffice Impress > General** and deselect **Start with Template Selection**.

Visual reference: the Options dialog under LibreOffice Impress > General, with the Start with Template Selection checkbox visible.

A fresh presentation starts with a single slide. To add more, go to **Slide > New Slide**, press *Ctrl+M*, or right-click in the Slides pane and choose **New Slide**. The new slide drops in right after whichever slide is currently selected. Need a copy instead? Right-click a slide and pick **Duplicate Slide**, or use **Slide > Duplicate Slide** from the menu bar.

Open the **Properties** panel on the Sidebar to configure slide format — screen ratio, orientation, background, and master slide are all there. The **Layouts** section underneath lets you pick a content arrangement. The first slide defaults to **Title Slide**; for the rest, choose whichever layout fits your content — text-only, two-column, chart, and so on.

Visual reference: the Sidebar Properties panel showing Slide format, orientation, background, and master slide dropdowns, with the Layouts section below.

Each layout provides content boxes you can click to fill: click the title area to type a title, click a text box to add body text, or use the placeholder icons to insert a table (**Insert > Table**), chart (**Insert > Chart**), image (**Insert > Image**), or audio/video (**Insert > Audio or Video**). You can always move or resize content boxes by dragging their selection handles in **Normal** view.

To reorder slides, just drag them to a new position in the Slides pane or in **Slide Sorter** view. For a polished feel, click **Slide Transition** on the Sidebar to pick transition effects between slides.

If you already have an outline in LibreOffice Writer, you can turn it into a deck directly. Open the Writer file and go to **File > Send > Outline to Presentation** — Impress creates one slide per top-level heading. Alternatively, use **File > Send > AutoAbstract to Presentation** to control how many outline levels and paragraphs per level get pulled in. Either way, you may want to duplicate or expand slides afterward to keep things digestible.

Visual reference: the Create AutoAbstract dialog with controls for included outline levels and paragraphs per level.

When you're ready to present, hit **Slide Show > Start from First Slide** (or just press *F5*). Use *Shift+F5* to start from the current slide instead.
