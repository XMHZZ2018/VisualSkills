# Process & Funnel Diagrams (LibreOffice Impress 7.3.7)

Process and funnel diagrams are built from basic shapes on the Drawing toolbar — mainly chevrons, pentagons, and block arrows. There's no dedicated wizard, but the shape tools make it straightforward.

Start by grabbing a **Chevron** from the **Block Arrows** category on the Drawing toolbar. Click the triangle ▼ next to the block arrow icon to open the sub-toolbar, then pick **Chevron** or **Pentagon**. Click and drag on the slide to place your first step shape. Hold **Shift** while dragging to keep equal height and width proportions.

Once you've got one chevron, duplicate it with **Ctrl+D** (or **Cmd+D** on macOS) and line the copies up horizontally. Use the yellow adjustment dot on each chevron to tweak the angle between the sides and fine-tune the pointed shape. Space them evenly by selecting all shapes, then using **Format > Position and Size** or the alignment tools.

To color each step, right-click a shape and choose **Area**. Pick a distinct fill color per stage — this is what makes the flow visually scannable. Add text by simply double-clicking a shape and typing your label; the text sits right inside.

For a funnel diagram, use **Basic Shapes > Trapezoid** shapes stacked vertically, each one narrower than the last. Rotate them if needed via **Format > Position and Size > Rotation**. Alternatively, draw custom funnel outlines using **Curves and Polygons** from the Drawing toolbar — click the triangle ▼ next to **Curves and Polygons**, select **Polygon**, and click to set each corner point.

Connectors between process steps work well too. Grab an arrow from **Lines and Arrows** on the Drawing toolbar — click **Insert Line** or pick a styled arrow. Hold **Ctrl** while drawing to snap endpoints to the nearest grid point.

To keep everything tidy, select all your diagram shapes and group them with **Format > Group > Group**. This locks the layout so nothing shifts when you move the diagram around the slide.
