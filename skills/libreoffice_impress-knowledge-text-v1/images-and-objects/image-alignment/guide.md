# Aligning & Distributing Objects (LibreOffice Impress 7.3.7)

Alignment tools let you line up two or more selected objects relative to each other. They only become active once you've selected multiple objects — so grab at least two before you start.

The alignment options break into two groups: **Left**, **Centered**, and **Right** control horizontal alignment, while **Top**, **Center**, and **Bottom** handle the vertical axis. Each one snaps the selected objects to match the corresponding edge or midpoint.

To access these, click the triangle next to **Align Objects** on the Line and Filling toolbar and pick an option. Alternatively, go to **View > Toolbars > Align Objects** to open a dedicated toolbar. You can also right-click your selection and choose **Align Objects** from the context menu.

The **Properties** sidebar offers quick alignment too. Select your objects, open the **Properties** deck, and expand the **Position and Size** panel — the **Align** row at the bottom has icon buttons for all six alignment options.

*Visual reference — Properties sidebar with the Position and Size panel expanded:* The Properties deck lists collapsed headers for **Character**, **Lists**, **Paragraph**, **Area**, **Shadow**, **Line**, and **Columns**, with the **Position and Size** panel expanded between them. Inside the expanded panel, numeric fields for **Position X**, **Position Y**, **Width**, and **Height** sit stacked with −/+ steppers on each, followed by a **Keep ratio** checkbox, a **Rotation** row with a small circular angle dial and a 0.00° field, a **Flip** row with two icons, an **Arrange** row with four ordering icons, and an **Align** row with six icon buttons (the three horizontal alignments and three vertical alignments). A vertical rail of sidebar-deck icons runs along the right edge, letting you switch between Properties, Styles, Gallery, Navigator, Shapes, Slide Transition, Animation, and Master Slide decks.

For even spacing, select three or more objects, right-click, and choose **Distribution**. This opens a dialog where you can distribute objects evenly along the horizontal axis (by left edge, center, spacing, or right edge) and the vertical axis (by top edge, center, spacing, or bottom edge). Pick the axes you need and hit **OK**.

When you need pixel-perfect placement, the snap grid helps. Turn it on via **View > Snap Guides > Snap to Grid**, or click **Snap to Grid** on the Options toolbar. Configure grid spacing and behavior under **Tools > Options > LibreOffice Impress > Grid**. You can also enable **Snap to Snap Guides** and **Snap to Object Border/Points** from the same menus for more snapping control.

*Visual reference — Options > LibreOffice Impress > Grid dialog:* A two-pane dialog titled **Options - LibreOffice Impress - Grid**. The left pane is a tree with top-level entries (**LibreOffice**, **Load/Save**, **Language Settings**, **LibreOffice Impress**, **LibreOffice Base**, **Charts**, **Internet**); under **LibreOffice Impress** the children **General**, **View**, **Grid**, **Print** are visible, with **Grid** highlighted orange. The right pane is organized in four sections. **Grid** has two checkboxes — **Snap to grid** (checked) and **Visible grid** (unchecked). **Resolution** has **Horizontal** and **Vertical** numeric fields (both 2.00 cm) with steppers. **Subdivision** has **Horizontal** and **Vertical** integer fields (both 4) with steppers and a "space(s)" label, followed by a **Synchronize axes** checkbox (checked). **Snap** has four checkboxes (**To snap lines**, **To the page margins**, **To object frame** all checked; **To object points** unchecked) and a **Snap range** field (5 pixels). **Constrain Objects** has **When creating or moving objects** (unchecked), **Extend edges** (checked), **When rotating** (unchecked, 15.00° field greyed), and **Point reduction** (15.00°).

For precise coordinates, press **F4** (or go to **Format > Object and Shape > Position and Size**) to open the **Position and Size** dialog, where you can type exact X/Y positions and dimensions. Check **Keep ratio** if you want proportional resizing.

*Visual reference — Position and Size dialog:* A modal dialog titled **Position and Size** with three tabs — **Position and Size** (selected, underlined in orange), **Rotation**, and **Slant & Corner Radius**. Under the selected tab, a **Position** section contains **Position X** (5.50 cm) and **Position Y** (3.50 cm) numeric fields with steppers, plus a **Base point** selector drawn as a 3×3 grid of radio dots (top-left dot selected) showing which corner or midpoint the X/Y values reference. A **Size** section below holds **Width** (6.00 cm) and **Height** (4.50 cm) fields, a **Keep ratio** checkbox (checked), and its own 3×3 base-point selector. A **Protect** group offers **Position** and **Size** checkboxes (both unchecked), and an **Adapt** group shows **Fit width to text** and **Fit height to text** (both greyed out). The standard button row at the bottom is **Help / Reset / Cancel / OK**.
