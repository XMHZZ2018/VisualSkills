# Using Collections and Tags (Zotero 7)

Zotero gives you two complementary ways to organize your library: **collections** (hierarchical folders) and **tags** (flat labels). Think of collections like playlists — the same item can live in multiple collections without being duplicated. Tags let you slice across collections, filtering by topic, status, or anything you dream up.

## Collections

To create a collection, click the **New Collection** button at the top of the left pane, or right-click **My Library** and choose **New Collection…**. You can nest them by right-clicking an existing collection and picking **New Subcollection…**, or just drag one collection onto another.

Adding items is drag-and-drop: select items in the center pane and drag them onto a collection. This *copies* them there — the originals stay put. To *move* instead, hold **Cmd** (Mac) or **Shift** (Windows/Linux) while dragging. Items saved from the browser automatically land in whatever collection is selected at the time.

To remove items from a collection without deleting them, select them and press **Delete** (or right-click and choose **Remove Item(s) from Collection…**). The items remain in **My Library**. To actually trash items, right-click and choose **Move Item(s) to Trash…**.

Deleting a collection itself (right-click → **Delete Collection…**) removes only the folder — the items inside are untouched. Use **Delete Collection and Items…** if you want both gone. Check the **Unfiled Items** virtual collection to spot anything that fell out of all collections.

## Tags

The **tag selector** sits at the bottom-left of Zotero. Click any tag there to filter the center pane to items carrying that tag; click again to deselect. Use the search box inside the selector to find tags by name.

To tag an item, select it, open the **Tags** tab in the right pane, and click **Add**. Type a name and press **Enter** — Zotero will autocomplete from existing tags. You can also drag items onto a tag in the tag selector to apply it in bulk.

Right-click a tag in the selector to **Rename Tag…** or **Delete Tag…** across your entire library. Renaming a tag to match an existing one merges them — handy for cleanup.

**Colored tags** are the real power move. Right-click a tag, choose **Assign Color…**, and pick a color. That tag then shows as a colored dot next to item titles and pins itself to the top of the tag selector. Each colored tag gets a number key (1–9) so you can toggle it on selected items instantly from the keyboard. Emoji tags work too — use an emoji as a tag name and it displays inline instead of a colored square.

Zotero may auto-import tags from databases when you save items. Hide these in the tag selector by clicking the **multi-color button** and unchecking **Show Automatic**, or disable them entirely in **Edit > Settings > General** by unchecking **Automatically tag items with keywords and subject headings**.
