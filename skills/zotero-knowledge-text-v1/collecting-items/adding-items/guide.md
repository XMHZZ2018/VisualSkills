# Adding Items to Your Library (Zotero 7)

The fastest way to get items into Zotero is the **Zotero Connector** — a browser extension for Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari. Install it alongside the desktop app, and you're set.

As you browse, the Connector's save button (in the browser toolbar) automatically detects what you're looking at. On a journal article page it shows an article icon; on a library catalog entry, a book icon. Just click it, and Zotero pulls in the metadata and often the PDF too. On search results pages (like Google Scholar), the button becomes a folder icon — click it to cherry-pick which items to save.

After clicking save, a popup lets you redirect the item to a specific collection or library and add tags on the fly.

If a page isn't recognized, the save button turns into a gray webpage icon. Clicking it saves a basic "Web Page" item with the title, URL, and access date. You can force this behavior on any page by right-clicking the save button and choosing **Save to Zotero (Web Page with/without Snapshot)**.

When you already have an ISBN, DOI, PubMed ID, arXiv ID, or ADS Bibcode, skip the browser entirely. Click the **Add Item by Identifier** button (the magic-wand icon) in the Zotero toolbar, paste in one or more identifiers separated by spaces or commas, and hit **Enter**. Zotero fetches the full metadata from the appropriate registry automatically.

For local files — say a PDF someone emailed you — just drag it into the Zotero center pane. Zotero saves it as a standalone attachment and tries to retrieve metadata. If that fails, right-click the attachment, choose **Create Parent Item**, and enter an identifier. If no identifier exists, click **Manual Entry** in that same dialog to fill in the details yourself.

As a last resort, you can create an item from scratch by clicking the green **New Item** button at the top of the middle pane and picking an item type. Fill in the bibliographic fields in the right-hand pane. But really, prefer the Connector or identifier lookup whenever possible — manual entry is slower and more error-prone.

Whichever method you use, always spot-check the imported metadata. Titles should be stored in sentence case, and author names should use full names (not just initials) so Zotero can disambiguate correctly in citations. Fix it once, and every future citation will be correct.
