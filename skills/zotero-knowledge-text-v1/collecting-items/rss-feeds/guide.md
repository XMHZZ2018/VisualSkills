# Subscribing to RSS Feeds (Zotero 7)

Feeds let you stay on top of new research from journals, publishers, or any source that offers an RSS feed. Zotero pulls in new items automatically so you can browse and selectively save what matters.

To subscribe, click the **New Library** button (the green plus icon above the left pane) and hover over **New Feed**. You'll see two options: **From URL…** and **From OPML…**.

The most common route is **From URL…**. Grab the RSS feed URL from the journal's website — look for the orange RSS icon near social media or email alert links, or just search for the journal name plus "RSS feed." Paste that URL into the **URL** field in the dialog that appears. If the URL is valid, Zotero will let you give the feed a custom **Title**. Expand **Advanced Options** to control how often the feed refreshes and how long read/unread items stick around before being purged.

If you're migrating from another reader like Feedly, choose **From OPML…** instead and point Zotero at your exported OPML file to import all your subscriptions at once.

Your feeds show up at the bottom of the left pane, below your libraries. Click any feed to browse its items — unread ones appear in **bold**. Select an item to preview its metadata, then hit **Mark As Unread** / **Mark As Read** (or press the **`** key) to toggle its status. To save something to your library, click **Add to "My Library"** or press **Ctrl+Shift+S** (**Cmd+Shift+S** on Mac). Use the dropdown arrow on that button to save directly into a group library instead.

One thing to note: read/unread status is local and won't sync across your devices.
