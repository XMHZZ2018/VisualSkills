# Creating Bibliographies Manually (Zotero 7)

Zotero gives you a few fast ways to generate formatted bibliographies outside of a word processor — handy for emails, blog posts, or quick sharing.

## Quick Copy (Drag-and-Drop)

The fastest method: select one or more items in your library's center column and drag them straight into any text field — an email draft, a Google Doc, a blog editor. Zotero drops in a fully formatted bibliography on the spot. If you need inline citations instead of full references, hold **Shift** as you start the drag.

To tweak the output style, go to **Zotero > Settings > Export**. There you can pick your default citation style, toggle HTML markup, and even set site-specific formats for particular journals or platforms.

You can also skip the drag entirely: select your items and press **Ctrl/Cmd-Shift-C** (or **Edit > Copy Bibliography**) to copy bibliography entries to your clipboard. For citations, use **Ctrl/Cmd-Shift-A** (or **Edit > Copy Citation**). Then just paste wherever you need them.

Quick Copy also handles export formats like BibTeX and RIS, so it's not limited to styled references.

## Right-Click Bibliography

For more control, select your items, right-click, and choose **Create Bibliography from Item(s)…**. A dialog lets you pick a citation style and toggle between **Citations/Notes** or **Bibliography** output. From there you can **Copy to Clipboard**, **Save as RTF**, **Save as HTML** (which embeds Zotero-readable metadata), or **Print** directly.

## RTF Scan

If you prefer writing with plain-text citation markers, Zotero's **RTF Scan** feature lets you draft freely and then convert those markers into properly formatted citations and a bibliography in your chosen style after the fact.
