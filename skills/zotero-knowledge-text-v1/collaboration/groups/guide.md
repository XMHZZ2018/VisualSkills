# Working with Groups (Zotero 7)

Zotero groups let you share libraries with collaborators — whether it's a seminar class, a research lab, or a loose network of peers. Group libraries live separately from your personal **My Library**, so they also double as a way to keep project-specific collections neatly isolated.

To create a group, go to **File → New Library → New Group…** in the Zotero desktop app. You can also create one from the groups page on zotero.org. Either way, you'll choose one of three types: **Private** (invisible to outsiders, hidden from searches), **Public, Closed Membership** (visible page, but joining requires an invitation), or **Public, Open Membership** (anyone can join instantly, though file sharing is disabled).

Once the group exists, manage it from its settings page on zotero.org. Under **Library Settings**, you control who can read, edit, and upload files — options range from "anyone on the internet" down to "only group admins." Under **Members Settings**, you can assign roles (member, admin, or owner) and send invitations via **Send More Invitations**.

A practical tip: if this is a lab or team group, create a dedicated Zotero account to act as the group owner, and make sure at least two people know the login. That way you won't lose access if someone leaves.

Back in the desktop client, enable **data syncing** and your group libraries will appear in the left pane under **Group Libraries**. Drag items from **My Library** into a group to share them — but remember, those become independent copies. Edits in the group won't update your personal copy unless you drag the item back.

To organize within a group, right-click the group library name and choose **New Collection…** or **New Saved Search…**. Collections work exactly as they do in your personal library.

Every group also gets a web page on zotero.org. Public groups use it as a portal for their shared work; private groups use it for browser-based access when you're away from your main machine. File storage in any group counts against the *owner's* storage quota, not the members'.
