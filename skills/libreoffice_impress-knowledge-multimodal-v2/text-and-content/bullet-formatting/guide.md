# Bullet List Formatting (LibreOffice Impress 7.3.7)

Impress gives you two contexts for lists: AutoLayout text boxes (which come with built-in outline styles) and plain text boxes you create yourself. The workflow differs slightly for each.

In an **AutoLayout** text box, just start typing — bullets are already active. The outline levels (1–9) map to presentation styles like Outline 2, Outline 3, etc. Demote a point with **Tab** or **Alt+Shift+Right Arrow**, and promote it back with **Shift+Tab** or **Alt+Shift+Left Arrow**. You can also use **Format > Lists** and pick **Promote** or **Demote** from the submenu.

For a **plain text box**, you need to turn bullets on first. Click **Toggle Unordered List** or **Toggle Ordered List** on the Text Formatting toolbar, or find the same toggles in the **Lists** panel of the Properties deck on the Sidebar. You can also go to **Format > Lists** and select **Unordered List** or **Ordered List**.

To reorder items without changing their indent level, use **Move Up** / **Move Down** — available on the Outline toolbar, the **Lists** panel in the Sidebar, or via **Format > Lists**. The shortcuts are **Alt+Shift+Up/Down Arrow**.

When you want to fine-tune the look, open the **Bullets and Numbering** dialog. Select your list text, then go to **Format > Lists > Bullets and Numbering**. On the left you pick which **Level** (1–10, or all at once with "1–10"). The **Properties** section lets you set the **Type** (bullet character, numbered, image), **Color**, **Rel. size**, and position settings like **Indent** and **Width**. Under **Scope**, choose whether changes apply to the whole **Slide** or just the current **Selection**, and hit **Apply to Master** if you want to push changes to the master slide.

For quick bullet-style switching, click the dropdown arrow next to **Toggle Unordered List** in the **Lists** panel or Text Formatting toolbar — this reveals a visual picker with various bullet characters (dots, arrows, diamonds, checkmarks, squares). Click **More Bullets** at the bottom for the full character set. The same dropdown on **Toggle Ordered List** shows numbering formats (1/2/3, a/b/c, I/II/III, etc.), with **More Numbering** for additional options.

Use **Shift+Enter** to start a new line within the same list point (no new bullet), and **Enter** to create the next point.
