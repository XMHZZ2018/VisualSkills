# Shape Rotation & Transformations (LibreOffice Impress 7.3.7)

To rotate a shape freehand, select it, then open the Transformations sub-toolbar by clicking the triangle next to **Transformations** on the Line and Filling toolbar and choosing **Rotate**. The selection handles change to round red handles at the corners — just grab one and drag to spin the object. Hold **Shift** while dragging to snap rotation to 15-degree increments, which is great for quick right-angle turns.

For precise rotation, open the **Position and Size** dialog with **F4** (or via **Format > Object and Shape > Position and Size**). Switch to the **Rotation** tab, set your exact degree value in the **Rotation Angle** field, adjust the **Pivot Point** if needed, and click **OK**. You can also do this from the Sidebar: click **Properties**, expand **Position and Size**, and type your angle directly into the **Rotation** field, then press **Enter**.

To flip a shape, right-click it and choose **Flip > Vertically** or **Horizontally**, or use **Format > Flip** from the menu bar. For more control, open the Transformations toolbar via **View > Toolbars > Transformations** and click **Flip** — this places a dashed symmetry axis through the object that you can reposition before dragging a handle across it to complete the flip. Hold **Shift** while moving the symmetry axis to lock it to 45-degree steps.

Impress doesn't have a true mirror command, but you can fake it: copy the object, flip the copy, paste it, then select both and use **Alignment** from the context menu to line them up with **Left**, **Center**, **Right**, **Top**, or **Bottom**.

For perspective effects, the Transformations toolbar offers **Distort**, **Set in Circle (perspective)**, and **Set to circle (slant)**. Select your object, click the tool, confirm the conversion to a curve if prompted, then drag the corner or edge handles to warp the shape. The perspective tools are especially handy for giving flat shapes a pseudo-3D look. Note that converting to a curve is one-way — use **Undo** if you change your mind.
