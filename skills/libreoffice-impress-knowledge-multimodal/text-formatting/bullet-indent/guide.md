# Bullets and Indentation (LibreOffice Impress 7.3.7)

## Creating Lists

List creation differs depending on text box type, but formatting tools are the same.

### AutoLayout Text Boxes

By default, AutoLayout text boxes use unordered (bulleted) lists based on outline styles. Read the screenshot `fig02.png` in this directory — it shows an example of the outline unordered list and the Outline toolbar.

1. Go to **View > Normal** on the Menu bar.
2. Select a slide layout via right-click context menu, **Slide > Layout**, or the Layouts panel in the Sidebar.
3. Click **"Click to add Text"** and type the first list item.
4. Press **Enter** to create a new list point; press **Shift+Enter** for a new line without a new list point.
5. To **demote** (increase indent level): press **Tab**, or **Alt+Shift+Right Arrow** (macOS: ⌥+Shift+Right Arrow), or click **Demote** on the Outline toolbar or Sidebar Lists panel, or go to **Format > Lists > Demote**.
6. To **promote** (decrease indent level): press **Shift+Tab**, or **Alt+Shift+Left Arrow** (macOS: ⌥+Shift+Left Arrow), or click **Promote** on the Outline toolbar or Sidebar Lists panel, or go to **Format > Lists > Promote**.
7. To **move a point down** without changing level: click **Move Down** on the Outline toolbar or Sidebar Lists panel, or go to **Format > Lists > Move Down**, or press **Alt+Shift+Down Arrow** (macOS: ⌥+Shift+Down Arrow).
8. To **move a point up** without changing level: click **Move Up** on the Outline toolbar or Sidebar Lists panel, or go to **Format > Lists > Move Up**, or press **Alt+Shift+Up Arrow** (macOS: ⌥+Shift+Up Arrow).

### Regular Text Boxes

1. Create a text box and ensure the cursor is active inside it.
2. Enable a list using one of:
   - Click **Toggle Unordered List** or **Toggle Ordered List** on the Text Formatting toolbar.
   - Click those buttons in the **Lists panel** in the Sidebar Properties deck.
   - Go to **Format > Lists > Unordered List** or **Ordered List**.
3. Press **Enter** for a new list point; **Shift+Enter** for a new line without a new point.
4. Indent/promote steps are the same as AutoLayout text boxes (steps 5–8 above).

## Formatting Lists

Read the screenshot `fig05.png` in this directory — it shows the Bullets and Numbering dialog and the Lists panel in the Sidebar.

- Use the **Bullets and Numbering dialog** to change list type, properties, size, position, and scope for the entire list or a single point.
- Use the **Lists panel** in the Sidebar Properties deck to change list type, promote/demote, or move points.
- Use the **Outline toolbar** to promote/demote or move points.
- Use the **Text Formatting toolbar** to change list type.
- Click the **▼ triangle** next to Toggle Unordered/Ordered List (toolbar or Sidebar) and select **More Bullets** or **More Numbering** to open extended bullet/numbering dialogs. Read the screenshot `fig06.png` in this directory — it shows the More Bullets and More Numbering dialogs.
