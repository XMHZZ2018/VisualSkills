# Subscribing to RSS Feeds (Zotero 7)

Zotero lets you subscribe to RSS feeds from journals, publishers, institutions, or any other source so you can keep up with new research right inside your library. When something interesting shows up, you can save it to your collection with a single click.

To add a feed, click the **New Library** button (the green plus icon) above the left pane and hover over **New Feed**. You'll see two options: **From URL…** and **From OPML…**.

See `fig01.png`.

The most common route is **From URL…**. Head to the journal or website you want to follow and look for the RSS icon — it's often tucked near social media links or email alert buttons. Copy that URL, paste it into the **URL** field in the Add Feed dialog, give it a title, and hit **Save**.

If you expand **Advanced Options** in that same dialog, you can control how often the feed refreshes (in hours) and how long read and unread items stick around before Zotero removes them automatically.

See `fig02.png`.

Already have a bunch of subscriptions in Feedly or another reader? Export them as an OPML file, then choose **From OPML…** instead to import them all at once.

Your feeds appear at the bottom of the left pane, below **My Library** and any group libraries. Click a feed to browse its items — unread ones show up in bold. Select an item to preview its metadata in the right pane.

See `fig03.png`.

To save a feed item into your library, click the **Add to "My Library"** button or press **Ctrl+Shift+S** (Mac: **Cmd+Shift+S**). Use the dropdown arrow on that button to save directly into a group library instead. You can toggle an item's read state with the **Mark As Unread/Read** button or the backtick key (**`**).

Right-click any feed in the left pane to manually refresh it, mark all its items as read, tweak its settings, or unsubscribe entirely. One thing to note: read/unread status doesn't sync across computers — it's local to each machine.
