# Adding Notes (Zotero 7)

Zotero lets you attach rich-text notes directly to items in your library — great for jotting down thoughts, excerpts, or reading annotations. Notes sync with your item metadata and don't count against your file storage quota, so use them freely.

**Child notes** are the most common type. Select an item in the center pane, then click the **New Note** button at the top of the center pane and choose **Add Child Note**. Alternatively, open the **Notes** tab in the right-hand pane and click **Add**, or simply right-click the item and select **Add Note**. The note appears as an attachment under that item.

Once created, a note editor opens in the right-hand pane with a formatting toolbar — bold, italic, lists, text color, and more. Your text saves automatically as you type. If you need more room, click the **Edit in a separate window** button at the bottom of the editor to pop the note out into its own window.

**Standalone notes** aren't tied to any particular item — they live alongside regular items in your library. To create one, click the **New Note** button and choose **New Standalone Note**. They work identically to child notes otherwise.

You can organize notes just like any other Zotero item. Scroll to the bottom of the note editor to find **Tags** and **Related** fields — click either one to tag the note or link it to other items in your library.

To search within a note, use **Ctrl/Cmd-F** or click the **Find and Replace** button in the editor. Notes are also included in Zotero's general search, so you can find them from the main search bar too.

Want to include visuals? Drag an image from your filesystem into the note editor, or paste one in. Keep in mind that embedded images *do* count against your Zotero Storage quota, unlike the note text itself.
